💡 Heart Health Tips
💧 Stay Hydrated
Dehydration can raise your resting heart rate by 5–8 BPM. Drink 2–3L of water daily.
🧘 Manage Stress
High stress elevates cortisol which increases heart rate. Practice deep breathing or yoga daily.
🏋️ Exercise Regularly
Consistent aerobic exercise lowers your resting heart rate over time — a sign of improved fitness.
⚠️ When to See a Doctor
If resting BPM is consistently above 100 or below 60 (without being an athlete), consult a physician.

💡 Sleep Improvement Tips
📱
No Screens 1 Hour Before Bed
Blue light from phones suppresses melatonin production by up to 50%, making it harder to fall asleep.
🌡️
Keep Your Room Cool (18–20°C)
A cooler room signals your body it's time to sleep. Use a fan or keep windows open.
⏰
Consistent Sleep Schedule
Going to bed and waking at the same time every day — even weekends — regulates your circadian rhythm.
☕
No Caffeine After 2 PM
Caffeine has a half-life of 5–6 hours. A 4 PM coffee still has half its caffeine active at 10 PM.
🧘
Relaxation Routine
10 minutes of deep breathing or meditation before bed reduces cortisol and helps you fall asleep faster.
